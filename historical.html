Liquid-Liquid Extraction: Naphthalene is significantly more soluble in organic solvents like hexane or dichloromethane than benzoic acid, which preferentially dissolves in water. This difference can be exploited for separation using a separatory funnel. Add the mixture to the chosen organic solvent and shake repeatedly with water. Naphthalene will partition into the organic layer, while benzoic acid remains in the aqueous layer. Separate the layers and evaporate the respective solvents to obtain crude fractions of each compound Differential Solubility: Benzoic acid has higher water solubility compared to naphthalene. Dissolving the mixture in hot water will preferentially dissolve benzoic acid, leaving naphthalene less soluble. Filter the hot solution to remove any undissolved naphthalene. Cool the filtrate to induce crystallization of benzoic acid. Separate the crystals and evaporate the remaining solution to recover any residual benzoic acid. The crude fractions obtained from the separation step may still contain impurities. Recrystallization using a suitable solvent will further purify each compound. Choose a solvent that dissolves the compound well at higher temperatures but has less solubility at lower temperatures. Common choices for naphthalene and benzoic acid include ethanol, methanol, or acetone. Dissolve the crude fraction in the chosen solvent at its boiling point. Filter the hot solution to remove any insoluble impurities. Allow the solution to cool slowly, preferably undisturbed, to encourage large, well-formed crystals. Collect the crystals by vacuum filtration and wash them with a small amount of cold solvent to remove adhering mother liquor. Finally, dry the crystals thoroughly under vacuum or on a watch glass.
